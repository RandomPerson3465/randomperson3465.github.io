Livecountsedit 4.1 was renamed to v5, and it is scheduled to release possibly around May 29-31. Please note that this is scheduled and the update hasn't even been finished yet. This means this could chnage in the future.

There are a few reasons for this.
1. RandomPerson3465 lives in the PST/PDT timezone, or UTC-8 and UTC-7 during daylight savings. He tends to release updates at around 3-11 PM so if you live in timezones UTC-2 or ahead add one day to release dates that don't have a release time. If there is a release time, just convert the time from PST/PDT to your timezone.
2. The update may have been delayed. Please check his Twitter for updates.
3. An unexpected event happened on the release date that causes him to not be able to release the update or notify you of it.

On versions before v4, and the old compare mode, all the options should be on the top left of the screen. On regular mode v4, click or tap the "Options" text at the bottom left corner of the screen, and on the new compare mode there should be a gear icon on the top left.

You are not required to give credit whenever you use Livecountsedit in your content.

If keybinds are not working, try focusing on the body of the website by clicking or tapping the background. This is to prevent them from triggering when typing out channel names, counts, etc. On the new compare mode, make sure keybinds are enabled in the settings.

Unfortunately YouTube's API has a lot of restrictions, so multiple people using this feature at once is more than enough to make the quota run out within an hour. Also, Livecountsedit is intended for making custom subscriber counts and not really for "estimations".

Livecountsedit v4 and the new compare mode have a different theme and different features because the old Livecountsedit was mostly copying off of livecounts.net's design, and the input boxes were taking up a lot of space. If lag is your issue, that may be due to the Highcharts graph so you can disable the chart in the options and see if that helps.
